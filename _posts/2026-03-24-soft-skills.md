## The Importance of Soft Skills in the Age of AI

More often, we are hearing of the great replacement of software engineers with agentic AI solutions. While I think this may be somewhat overblown, the truth is
software engineers, to varying degrees of efficacy, are employing artificial intelligence to write code, effectively outsourcing a lot of busy work to automation.

The skills that will prove to be more important than coding will ultimately be soft skills, primarily communication, prioritization, organization and planning. 
Effective leadership requires not only communicating specific needs to all parties, but to artificial intelligence which can be prone to hallucination. Much like humans,
AI needs clear direction and an effective human technologist will require a clearer sense of business needs, and will live closer to the product than the code.

While many programmers may bemoan the death of their skill to artificial intelligence, they should equally welcome the freeing of their time and energy for more noble pursuits
within their organizations - focusing on product design/development and how it can more accurately meet the needs of the business. It will allow us coders to build more
realistic products that meet the needs of the real world, while leaving the optimization, abstraction and heavy lifting to artificial intelligence (the annoying bits). In other words,
we provide artificial intelligence with oversight and accountability, leaving human elements of the business to humans. If we can dream of such a future, maybe a terminator style doomsday
scenario can be avoided.
