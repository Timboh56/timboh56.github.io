### Data Interoperability

Running a startup in the healthcare space is a bit like flying the millenium falcon in an asteroid field - obstacles and traps lay about that are typically not present in most technology companies. Regulatory hurdles, constantly changing guidelines, and specific implementations from your clients can challenge your ability to innovate and be competitive, but it also allows a lot of room for growth and opportunity. At the least, having reliable and up to date data, or data interoperability, is one of the biggest challenges faced in healthcare today. Simply put, data interoperability describes the exchange, availability, and reliability of data between separate healthcare parties, between hospitals, physicians, insurance companies and payers. Simple APIs don't exist and making sure your data is reliable and up to date requires many custom integrations with clients using a variety of methods, like file reads from uploads on a SFTP server. Raw data is then needed to be transformed to be applicable to your startup's architecture and needs.

### Complexity

The complexities of private healthcare also presents a unique challenge in healthcare - each plan, each client, each payer or party may have a different integration or setup that is brokered on an individual scale. As programmers, we try to abstract complexities into broader ideas and patterns, but the complexities and specificities of healthcare systems can hinder this.
